The Terracotta Army shows the military scale and political ambition of the Qin empire.

The Terracotta Army is one of the most important historical sites associated with Xi'an. It is not only a famous tourist attraction, but also a major archaeological discovery that helps explain how Qin power was organised, represented, and preserved for the afterlife. For a city-promotion website, this landmark gives Xi'an strong historical authority on an international level.

Connection with the Qin Dynasty

The site is closely linked with Qin Shi Huang, the first emperor who unified China. Because of that connection, the Terracotta Army is often discussed not only as sculpture, but as evidence of early imperial government, military power, and state ambition.

Imperial symbolism

The army was created to accompany the emperor in the afterlife, which immediately shows the scale of royal authority in the Qin period. The number of figures, their arrangement, and their realistic design all reflect a state that wanted to project discipline and permanence.

Political meaning

In coursework terms, the Terracotta Army is useful because it allows the website to explain Xi'an through political history, not only through sightseeing. It gives concrete visual evidence for discussing centralisation, military strength, and imperial rule.

Archaeological and cultural value

The importance of the site goes far beyond tourism. It contributes to historical knowledge, museum studies, and the global image of Chinese civilisation.

Why scholars and visitors care

The figures are remarkable because they present a large, organised visual record of the Qin military world. Differences in posture, armour, and rank also help visitors understand that the site is not decorative alone. It records hierarchy, planning, and a strong imperial worldview.
