Promoting My Hometown City

Xi'an is where ancient history still shapes everyday city life.

Xi'an is one of the most historically significant cities in China. As an ancient capital with more than three thousand years of urban history, it is known for the Terracotta Army, the City Wall, the Bell Tower, and the Big Wild Goose Pagoda. At the same time, it remains a lively modern city filled with local food culture, tourism, universities, and growing urban energy.

Why Xi'an deserves to be promoted

Xi'an deserves promotion because it combines national historical importance with a vivid modern urban character. Its landmarks are widely recognised, yet the city also feels approachable through food culture, public space, and everyday local life.

A city with historical depth

Xi'an served as the capital of several important dynasties in Chinese history. Because of this, the city still carries a strong sense of cultural continuity and national importance.

Four landmarks that represent Xi'an

These places together explain why Xi'an is remembered as a city of both heritage and presence.

Terracotta Army

It gives visitors a direct and unforgettable sense of Qin dynasty scale, discipline, and historical power.

Ancient City Wall

The wall is one of Xi'an’s most iconic features and helps define the city’s historical structure.

Big Wild Goose Pagoda

This landmark connects religious history, Tang culture, and modern tourism in a single image.

Bell Tower

It anchors the city centre and gives Xi'an one of its clearest urban night identities.
